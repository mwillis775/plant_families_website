Rhachidosoraceae

Rhachidosorus Fern Family

Rhachidosoraceae is a small family of ferns in the order Polypodiales, containing only the genus Rhachidosorus with approximately 8 species. These elegant ferns are characterized by their large, finely divided fronds and distinctive elongated sori along the veins. Native to East and Southeast Asia, they typically inhabit moist forest environments at moderate elevations. Previously classified within Athyriaceae, molecular studies have confirmed their status as a distinct family.

Overview

The Rhachidosoraceae family is a small but distinctive group of ferns represented by a single genus, Rhachidosorus. These elegant terrestrial ferns are characterized by their finely divided fronds and unique sori arrangement. They are primarily distributed across East and Southeast Asia, from Japan and Korea to the Philippines and parts of Malesia.

Rhachidosorus species are typically found in moist forest understories and along shaded mountain slopes, often at mid-elevations between 500-2000 meters. Their most distinctive feature is the arrangement of elongated sori that follow the veins on the underside of their fronds, often in a curved or J-shaped pattern.

Molecular phylogenetic studies have confirmed Rhachidosoraceae as a distinct lineage within the large fern order Polypodiales, though they were previously classified within Athyriaceae based on morphological similarities.

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Rhachidosoraceae
  • Common Name: Rhachidosorus Fern Family
  • Number of Genera: 1 (Rhachidosorus)

  • Evolutionary Group: Monilophytes - Leptosporangiate Ferns - Polypodiales

Key Characteristics

Growth Form and Habit

Terrestrial ferns with short-creeping to erect rhizomes covered in scales. Plants typically form clumps or grow as scattered individuals in forest understory.

Fronds

Fronds are large (often 50-100 cm long), finely divided (bipinnate to tripinnate), and typically have a broadly lanceolate to ovate outline. The stipes (stems) are long with scales concentrated at the base. The lamina (blade) is thin-textured but firm.

Pinnae and Pinnules

Multiple pairs of pinnae (primary divisions) arranged along the rachis, often with acuminate tips. Pinnules (secondary divisions) are typically lobed or further divided, with serrated margins on the ultimate segments.

Reproductive Structures

The most distinctive feature of Rhachidosoraceae is their elongated sori that run along the veins on the underside of fertile fronds. These sori are often curved or J-shaped and are covered by linear indusia that are attached along one side of the sorus.

Like all ferns, they reproduce via spores rather than seeds, and exhibit alternation of generations between the dominant sporophyte (the visible fern plant) and the small, heart-shaped gametophyte that develops from spores.

Distinguishing Features

Rhachidosoraceae can be distinguished from related fern families by their combination of:

  • Finely divided fronds with a specific architecture
  • Distinctive elongated sori following the veins
  • Linear indusia attached along one side
  • Molecular characteristics that place them as a distinct lineage

Identification

Field Identification Tips

When trying to identify members of the Rhachidosoraceae family in the field, look for:

  • Large, finely divided fronds with a bipinnate to tripinnate structure
  • Elongated sori running along veins on the underside of fronds, often with a curved or J-shaped pattern
  • Linear indusia attached along one side of the sorus
  • Terrestrial growth habit in moist forest understory
  • Scales present on rhizome and base of stipe
  • Typically found in East and Southeast Asian forests at moderate elevations

Similar Families

Rhachidosoraceae may be confused with several related fern families:

  • Athyriaceae: Similar in overall appearance but with different sori arrangement and molecular characteristics. Athyrium species often have shorter, more curved sori.
  • Aspleniaceae: Has linear sori but typically with different frond architecture and undivided indusia. Asplenium species often have simpler frond divisions.
  • Woodsiaceae: Different indusium structure (often cup-shaped) and overall morphology.
  • Dryopteridaceae: Often more leathery fronds with different sori arrangement, typically round rather than elongated.

Distinguishing Features

The most reliable features for distinguishing Rhachidosoraceae from similar ferns include:

  • Sori arrangement: Elongated along veins in a distinctive pattern
  • Frond architecture: Specific pattern of division that is highly dissected
  • Indusium attachment: Linear and attached along one side of the sorus
  • Geographic distribution: Primarily East and Southeast Asian

For definitive identification, examination of the sori and indusia under magnification is often necessary, as these features are the most diagnostic for the family.

Notable Examples

The family Rhachidosoraceae contains only the genus Rhachidosorus with approximately 8 species. Here are some of the most notable representatives:

Rhachidosorus mesosorus

Japanese Rhachidosorus Fern

The type species of the genus, native to Japan, Korea, and parts of China. Features large, finely divided fronds with characteristic elongated sori along the veins. The fronds are typically bipinnate to tripinnate with acuminate pinnae tips. Typically found in moist forest understory at moderate elevations, often growing near streams or in ravines.

Rhachidosorus consimilis

Chinese Rhachidosorus Fern

Native to southern China, this species features bipinnate to tripinnate fronds with finely serrated margins on the ultimate segments. The sori are elongated and slightly curved, following the veins on the underside of the frond. Distinguished from R. mesosorus by subtle differences in frond architecture and the shape of the pinnules.

Rhachidosorus siamensis

Thai Rhachidosorus Fern

A more recently described species from Thailand (2012), featuring distinctive frond architecture and sori arrangement. Found in mountainous regions of northern Thailand in moist forest habitats. This species represents an important range extension for the genus and has contributed to our understanding of the family's diversity and distribution.

Phylogenetic Note

Rhachidosoraceae was previously included within Athyriaceae based on morphological similarities, but molecular phylogenetic studies have confirmed it as a distinct evolutionary lineage. It is part of the large eupolypod II clade within the Polypodiales order, which includes most of the more advanced fern families.
